üìÑ Sample Answer (For Guidance Only ‚Äì Do Not Copy Word for Word)

For this company of around 50 employees, my detection and monitoring plan focuses on the most critical systems and identities. I would monitor endpoint logs from all workstations and laptops, particularly EDR events and Windows Security logs, to detect malware, suspicious processes, and local privilege escalation. At the network layer, I would collect firewall, VPN, and DNS logs to detect unusual outbound connections, lateral movement, and access from unexpected regions. In the cloud, I would enable detailed audit and sign-in logs for our identity provider (e.g., Azure AD or Google Workspace) and email security platform to spot phishing, login anomalies, and risky mailbox rules. To analyze these signals, I would deploy a SIEM to aggregate logs from endpoints, network devices, and cloud services, and an EDR/XDR solution to provide real-time visibility and response on endpoints. An IDS/IPS at the perimeter or in the cloud would add another layer of network-based detection. To reduce false positives, I would tune detection rules over time, whitelist known safe behavior, and use correlation across multiple data sources instead of reacting to single events in isolation. To reduce false negatives, I would ensure full log coverage for all critical assets, keep signatures and analytics updated, and include behavioral detections. This approach is aligned with NIST SP 800-61 and SP 800-137 by supporting continuous monitoring, early detection, and effective incident response.
