NIST IR ‚Äì Detection & Analysis SOC Monitoring

üö¶ Incident Triage ‚Äì Severity, Volatility, Impact

From ‚ÄúAlert‚Äù to ‚ÄúIncident‚Äù

Prioritisation NIST CSF ‚Äì Detect

Incident triage is the process of rapidly deciding what to do first. Not every alert deserves the same attention. Triage looks at:

  • Severity ‚Äì What is the potential business impact if this is real?
  • Evidence volatility ‚Äì What data will disappear soon (memory, network flows)?
  • Scope ‚Äì How many users, systems, or data sets are affected?

üì¶ Evidence Collection ‚Äì Photography, Labeling, Chain of Custody

Doing Forensics the Right Way

NIST SP 800-86 Integrity & Chain of Custody

Evidence collection is more than ‚Äúgrabbing a disk image‚Äù. It includes:

  • Documentation & labeling ‚Äì Who collected what, where, when, and how.
  • Photography ‚Äì Physical scene, cable layout, device labels, serial numbers.
  • Forensic acquisition ‚Äì Using trusted tools to capture bit-level copies or logical exports.
  • Chain of custody ‚Äì Documenting every handoff so evidence remains admissible.

NIST SP 800-86 describes the forensics phases: Collection ‚Üí Examination ‚Üí Analysis ‚Üí Reporting, with strong emphasis on preserving integrity.

üõ°Ô∏è Respond Function ‚Äì Containment, Mitigation, Communication

From Detection to Action

Containment Eradication Recovery

The Respond function covers the actions taken once an incident is confirmed:

  • Containment ‚Äì Stop lateral movement and damage (isolate hosts, block accounts, segment networks).
  • Eradication ‚Äì Remove malware, backdoors, or misconfigurations.
  • Recovery ‚Äì Restore systems and data, monitor for reinfection.
  • Communication ‚Äì Coordinate with leadership, legal, PR, and affected stakeholders.

NIST‚Äôs incident response lifecycle frames this as Detection & Analysis ‚Üí Containment, Eradication, and Recovery ‚Üí Post-incident Activity.

‚úÖ Validation Methods ‚Äì Separating False Positives from Real Threats

Trust but Verify

Alert Validation Detection Tuning

Validation is where you prove that an alert is a true incident (or safely dismiss it) using additional data:

  • Cross-checking sources ‚Äì SIEM alert + EDR telemetry + firewall logs.
  • Context ‚Äì Is this behavior normal for this user/host at this time?
  • Threat intelligence ‚Äì Does the IP/hash/domain appear in threat feeds?
  • Reproducibility ‚Äì Can you replay the query or test the rule on historical data?

Effective validation reduces alert fatigue by cutting false positives, while tuning detections reduces false negatives.
